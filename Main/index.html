01. Background

About

Nourhan's research trajectory is characterized by a deep engagement with the theoretical foundations of general relativity and quantum mechanics. Her academic work has focused on developing analytical and computational approaches to understanding black hole physics, particularly the mechanisms underlying Hawking radiation and the thermodynamic behavior of event horizons.

During her undergraduate years, she conducted extensive independent research culminating in a comprehensive paper on Hawking radiation and black hole thermodynamics. This work synthesized concepts from quantum field theory, statistical mechanics, and general relativity to explore the entropy-area relationship and the information paradox that has captivated theoretical physicists for decades.

Her research experience includes computational modeling of astrophysical systems, participation in seminar series on gravitational physics, and collaboration with faculty on problems in orbital mechanics and spacecraft dynamics. Nourhan has developed expertise in both analytical techniques for solving Einstein's field equations and numerical methods for simulating complex gravitational phenomena.

Beyond formal research, she maintains an active presence in scientific communication through her digital archive at shatanourhan.space, where she explores the philosophical and theoretical implications of modern astrophysics.

02. Research Areas

Research Interests

Nourhan's research program centers on the quantum aspects of black hole physics and their implications for our understanding of spacetime structure. Her work spans theoretical analysis, computational modeling, and the development of frameworks for testing quantum gravity predictions.

⚫

Black Hole Thermodynamics

Investigation of thermodynamic properties of black holes, including entropy calculations, temperature relations, and the application of statistical mechanics to horizons. Her work explores connections between Bekenstein-Hawking entropy and quantum information theory.

🌊

Hawking Radiation

Analysis of quantum field theory in curved spacetime, focusing on particle creation near event horizons and the mechanisms of black hole evaporation. Her research examines semiclassical approximations and their limitations.

🔬

Quantum Gravity

Exploration of quantum aspects of gravitational phenomena, including the information paradox, holographic principles, and attempts to reconcile quantum mechanics with general relativity at the Planck scale.

🛸

Spacecraft Dynamics

Application of relativistic mechanics to spacecraft trajectory optimization, with emphasis on missions in extreme gravitational fields and the engineering challenges of probing near-horizon physics.

03. Research Output

Publications & Papers

Hawking Radiation and Black Hole Thermodynamics: A Comprehensive Analysis of Quantum Effects Near Event Horizons
Shata Nourhan
Preprint (2026) • Independent Research Paper
This paper presents a detailed analysis of Hawking radiation as a quantum phenomenon arising from vacuum fluctuations near black hole event horizons. Beginning with the derivation of the Bekenstein-Hawking entropy formula, the work explores the thermodynamic properties of black holes and their implications for quantum gravity. The analysis includes semiclassical calculations of black hole temperature, discussions of the information paradox, and potential resolutions through holographic principles. Applications to astrophysical black holes and connections to quantum field theory in curved spacetime are examined in depth.
