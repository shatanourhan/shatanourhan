Entropy Evolution in Stationary Black Hole Spacetimes: A Precursor to Quantum Thermodynamics of Gravity

    1. Background & Motivation

    Black hole thermodynamics establishes deep parallels between the laws of mechanics and the laws of thermodynamics. The Bardeen–Carter–Hawking framework (1973) and subsequent work by Bekenstein and Hawking showed that a black hole’s surface gravity, area, and mass correspond respectively to temperature, entropy, and energy. Yet, the statistical and quantum underpinnings of this entropy remain partly mysterious — particularly when approaching non-equilibrium or near-extremal conditions.
    This project aims to revisit the classical and semiclassical derivations of black hole entropy and its variations under small perturbations, as a conceptual and technical bridge toward Quantum Thermodynamics of Gravity (QTDG). The intention is to establish a clear, mathematically explicit foundation that connects geometric mechanics to thermodynamic language, ready for quantum extension in future work.

    2.Objectives

    1.Model Setup: Formulate the stationary, axisymmetric spacetime model (e.g. Kerr or Reissner–Nordström) and re-derive its entropy–area relation from the first law of black hole mechanics.
    2.Entropy Variation: Compute entropy changes (ΔS_{BH}) under infinitesimal energy or angular momentum loss and estimate ΔS_{rad} for emitted radiation using semiclassical approximations.
    3.Near-Extremal Limit: Analyze deviations in the entropy balance near the extremal condition and identify possible mismatches between classical and semiclassical formulations.
    4.Interpretation: Examine the thermodynamic consistency of these results and discuss their implications for emerging frameworks in Quantum Thermodynamics of Gravity.

    4. Expected Outcome (Seed Phase)

    -A self-contained derivation and analysis note linking classical black hole mechanics to thermodynamic entropy flow.
    -Identification of conceptual and quantitative gaps that motivate a full MPhil project in Quantum Thermodynamics of Gravity.
    -A small, standalone theoretical paper (or preprint) demonstrating technical competence in general relativity and black hole thermodynamics.

    Realistic outcomes in 6 months:

    -A complete, self-contained research paper (10–15 pages).
    -A correct derivation using the Kerr–Newman metric in the near-extremal limit.
    -A clearly stated deviation or correction (main contribution).
    -Submission to arXiv and possibly a mid-tier open-access journal (Entropy, Universe, Symmetry, etc.).

    5.Future Connection

    The results of this preliminary study will inform a deeper investigation of entropy production, information flow, and coarse-graining in gravitational systems.
    This follow-up (MPhil) stage will integrate tools from quantum thermodynamics, such as open quantum system models and entanglement-based entropy, to explore the microphysical origins of the Bekenstein–Hawking law and its generalizations.
